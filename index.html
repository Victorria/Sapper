Rules

Game: Sapper

The objective of Sapper is to uncover all the tiles on a grid without detonating any hidden mines.

To play, you can use a mouse (left and right buttons), a keyboard (Enter, and "m" keys; Tab or Shift + Tab), or a touch screen device.

Equipment:

  • A grid of tiles, initially covered.
  • Mines hidden beneath some of the tiles.

Icons:

- a closed tile
- no mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 1 mine in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 2 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 3 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 4 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 5 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 6 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 7 mines in the neighbor tiles
- exactly 8 mines in the neighbor tiles

Buttons:

or - the buttons that toggle the functions of the left and right mouse buttons

Gameplay:

A player can perform one of two actions:
a) Uncover a tile: Choose a covered tile to reveal its content.
b) Flag a tile: If a player suspects a tile contains a mine, they can flag it with another mouse button as .
If a player uncovers a tile with a mine, the game ends, and they lose.
If a player uncovers a tile without a mine, the tile will display a number indicating the number of neighbor tiles that contain mines.
If a player uncovers a tile with a number, they can use that information to deduce the location of nearby mines.


Game is finished when:

a) All tiles except the ones containing mines are uncovered: The player wins.
b) A player uncovers a tile with a mine: The player loses.
Flagging Tiles:
Players can flag a tile if they believe it contains a mine.
Flagged tiles cannot be uncovered unless the player removes the flag.
Additional Gameplay Option:
Players can choose to start a new game with a different grid size.
